Applied Artificial Intelligence for Public Safety and Security Workshop on Big Data, 2025 (AI-PublicSecurity-2025) held with 2025 IEEE International Conference on Big Data, Macau – December 8–11, 2025



    Call for Papers

    The workshop is anticipated to enhance public safety and security through the exploration of theoretical and practical aspects related to the application and algorithms of artificial intelligence in combating criminal activities, whether in the real or virtual worlds. The workshop affords opportunities and platforms for industry partners, government agents, and academic scientists to present novel applications, advanced solutions, state-of-the-art algorithms in the domain of public safety and security. Criminal activities jeopardize public safety with threats to societal well-being in the real world. Law Enforcement Agencies (LEA) must adapt and employ new methods and approaches to detect, prevent, and control criminal activities, thereby enhancing situational awareness in investigative or law enforcement functions, tactical situations, unexpected attacks, and responses to crimes in progress. We invite paper submissions on theories and applications of new emerging technologies aimed at enhancing public safety and security. Topics of interest for this workshop include :
      I. Artificial Intelligence
      1. Adversarial Attacks in public safety and Security
      2. Neural networks for improving public safety and security.
      3. Data mining in Public Safety and security
      4. Applications of Deep learning and machine learning for improving situational awareness
      5. The Application of deep Learning and Machine Learning in the Context of Public Safety.
      6. Data Intelligence and Anomaly Detection in Public Safety
      7. Simulations and Modeling in Public Safety
      8. Data Privacy in Public Safety
      9. Preventing Human trafficking
      10. Preventing High-risk medication
      11. Security Games and Game Theory
      12. Early Warning and Preparedness in Public Safety
      13. Search and Rescue in Public Safety
      14. Damage Assessment in Public Safety


      II. Big Data
      1. Data Quality in Public Safety
      2. Entity Resolution in Public Safety
      3. Data governance in Public Safety
      4. Data Visualization in Public Safety
      5. Novel theories, simulations, and applications of data quality, visualization, and data governance
      6. GPS trajectory data sets, aerial images, and satellite observation data enhancing public safety and security.
      7. Survey and crowdsourcing datasets for enhancing public safety and security.

      Call for Committee Members

      We invite researchers who have been active in big data and artificial intelligence to volunteer their efforts and serve on the AI-PublicSecurity-2025 committee (PC). The quality of workshop depends heavily on the paper review process which is performed by the PC members. We hope that this call will allow us to attract people with complementary expertise to join the PC and enhance public safety and security through artificial intelligence. Note that there will not be any face-to- face PC meeting and all reviews will be handled electronically. We expect that each member of the PC will review 3-4 papers on average. The review period will be between July 1 and November 1, 2025. In recognition of your efforts, your name will be mentioned in the workshop website. To be considered, please provide the following information to weidai [AT] pnw.edu:
    • Your name, affiliation, institutional/company email address, and short bio
    • List one to three topics that best represent the focus of your research and expertise
    • List relevant topic where you have been a member of the Program Committee
